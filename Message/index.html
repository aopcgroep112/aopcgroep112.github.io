    Message

    Direct or indirect approach?

    Use direct when the audience is receptive, eager, pleased, neutral,...

    Use indirect when the audience will be resistant to the message, displeased, uninterested, unwilling to cooperate,..

    Indirect: introduce yourself, refer to WIIFT, build interest/credibility,...

    Tip

    Put your main points/ideas at the beginning and end of your message!

    Tip

    Use an indirect approach when bringing bad news!

    Pick a medium!

    Written (document, text,...)

    Positive: permanent and accesible, convenient, can be edited, time saving

    Negative: difficult to convey emotions, who will read it and when?, no immediate feedback

    Important: follow up your communication, it's hard to build personal relationships using the written medium

    Oral (telephone, conference call,...)

    Positive: can convey emotion, no permanent record

    Negative: less precise, no permanent record, not much detail

    Important: if not face-to-face, there is no body language

    Blended (words and non-verbal cues, meeting, presentation + handout,...)

    Positive: group relationship, control if/how/when the message is received, immediate feedback

    Negative: time consuming, less precise, no permanent record
